## ğŸ“ COACH IMPLEMENTATION GUIDE

### **Session 1: Belief Mapping (20 minutes)**

**Objectives:**
1. Identify 3-5 limiting beliefs player holds
2. Explain the Why Ladder process
3. Begin first worksheet together

**Your Script:**
> "You've done great work identifying beliefs that might be holding you back. Now we're going to test these like scientists test hypotheses. We'll gather evidence, create experiments, and see what's actually true. This isn't about positive thinking - it's about accurate thinking based on reality."

**Action Steps:**
1. Have player list limiting beliefs
2. Rate conviction (0-10) for each
3. Pick ONE to work on first (usually most impactful)
4. Complete Steps 1-2 of worksheet together
5. Assign completion of worksheet before next session

---

### **Session 2-3: Evidence Examination (15 min each)**

**Objectives:**
1. Review completed worksheet
2. Analyze evidence together
3. Identify cognitive distortions
4. Co-create alternative belief

**Key Questions to Ask:**

**About Evidence:**
- "You listed 2 examples FOR and 4 AGAINST. What does that tell you?"
- "Are these reasons ALWAYS true or just SOMETIMES?"
- "Which of these factors can you control?"

**About Cognitive Distortions:**
- "Is this all-or-nothing thinking?" (I ALWAYS lose vs I've lost some)
- "Are you filtering out positive evidence?" (Ignoring wins, focusing on losses)
- "Are you predicting the future based on small sample size?"
- "Are you taking responsibility for things outside your control?"

**About Alternative Beliefs:**
- "What would be a more accurate way to think about this?"
- "How would your favorite pro player think about this situation?"
- "If this belief weren't true, how would you approach the next match?"

**Attributional Analysis:**
Add these questions for each "why":

| Question | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| "Is this ALWAYS true or just SOMETIMES?" | Assess stability (stable beliefs are more limiting) |
| "Is this something you can INFLUENCE or change?" | Assess controllability (uncontrollable beliefs create helplessness) |
| "Is this about YOU, the SITUATION, or OTHER PEOPLE?" | Assess locus (internal vs external attribution) |

**Common Attribution Patterns to Watch:**

| Pattern | Example | Issue | Better Alternative |
|---------|---------|-------|-------------------|
| Internal-Stable-Uncontrollable | "I'm just not a closer" | Learned helplessness | "I'm learning to manage pressure in closing situations" |
| External-Stable-Uncontrollable | "Experienced players always beat me" | Removes agency | "Experience is one factor; I control my preparation and execution" |
| Internal-Unstable-Controllable | "I didn't execute my tactics today" | Optimal attribution | Keep this! |

---

### **Session 4+: Behavioral Experiments**

**Before Match/Practice:**
1. Review the belief being tested
2. Confirm self-talk reminder and physical cue
3. Make specific prediction: "If old belief is true, what will happen?"

**Example:**
- Old belief: "I can't close after winning first set"
- Prediction: "If this is true, I'll tighten up at 4-3 in the second set and lose"
- Test: Next match where this situation occurs
- Cue: Racquet tap + "Process, not protect"

**After Match/Practice:**
Use structured debrief (see below)

## ğŸ—£ï¸ STRUCTURED DEBRIEF PROTOCOL

### **Scenario 1: New Belief Worked Well**

**Questions to Ask:**
1. "What specifically did you do differently?"
2. "What was your self-talk in the key moments?"
3. "Rate 0-10: How much do you believe the OLD belief now?"
4. "What evidence did today provide AGAINST the old belief?"

**Action:**
- Update conviction rating in worksheet
- Celebrate specific behaviors (not just outcome)
- Identify next situation to practice

**Warning:** Don't stop here just because one match went well. Need consistent evidence over multiple matches.

---

### **Scenario 2: Old Pattern Showed Up Again**

**CRITICAL: Prevent Confirmation Bias**

**Questions to Ask:**
1. "Did the belief CAUSE the outcome, or did other factors contribute?"
   - Opponent raised level?
   - Physical fatigue?
   - Tactical errors unrelated to mindset?

2. "Were there ANY moments where you DID use the new perspective?"
   - Even 2-3 points counts as progress

3. "Was this a complete confirmation of the old belief, or more nuanced?"

4. "What percentage of the match did you implement the new approach?"

**Example Reframe:**
âŒ **Avoid:** "See, you lost the second set again - the belief is true"

âœ… **Use:** "You held serve twice while staying aggressive - that's progress. The set was competitive (4-6), not a collapse. Your opponent also raised their level. This shows you CAN compete in second sets, even if this specific one didn't go your way. That's different from 'can't close matches.'"

**Action:**
- Update worksheet with nuanced evidence
- Identify specific moment where pattern activated
- Plan adjustment for next test

---

### **Scenario 3: Belief Seems Resistant to Change**

**If after 3-4 matches, conviction hasn't dropped 2+ points:**

**Go Deeper:**
1. "What would it MEAN about you if this belief weren't true?"
   - May uncover identity belief: "I'm not a closer" = "I'm not elite"

2. "Is there a part of you that wants to keep this belief?"
   - Secondary gain: Low expectations = less pressure to perform

3. "Who else believes this about you? Did someone tell you this?"
   - Parent? Previous coach? Internalized external voice?

**ğŸš© Red Flags for Referral:**
- Belief connects to self-worth: "I'm worthless if I lose"
- Generalization beyond tennis: "I fail at everything"
- Significant anxiety/avoidance: Won't compete because of belief
- Eating/sleep disruption related to belief
- Self-harm thoughts connected to performance

**If red flags present:** Refer to licensed psychologist while continuing performance work within scope.

## ğŸ”„ INTEGRATION WITH TENNIS TRAINING

### **How to Practice New Beliefs On-Court:**

**Don't just talk about beliefs - CREATE situations where old belief would activate**

**Example 1: "Can't close matches after winning first set"**

**Practice Design:**
- Play practice sets where player gets 5-0 lead
- Coach calls timeout at 5-2: "This is where your belief says you tighten up"
- Player states new self-talk out loud
- Continue set with focus on process cues
- Debrief: Did old pattern activate? Did new perspective help?

**Example 2: "Experienced players beat me"**

**Practice Design:**
- Have player compete against older/more experienced training partner
- Before: "What's your prediction if the old belief is true?"
- During: Focus on controllables (serve placement, footwork, point construction)
- After: "What did you control regardless of opponent's experience?"

**Example 3: "I choke under pressure"**

**Practice Design:**
- Create consequence-based games (loser runs, winner chooses drill)
- Notice physical activation (heart rate up, hands sweaty)
- Practice new self-talk: "Pressure means this matters - channel it"
- Track performance under pressure vs. practice

## ğŸ’¡ FINAL COACHING TIPS

1. **Don't Rush:** Belief change takes 6-12 weeks. One good match doesn't mean belief is gone.

2. **Celebrate Process:** Praise evidence-gathering and questioning, not just outcomes.

3. **Expect Resistance:** Beliefs exist for reasons (protection, low expectations = less pressure). Be patient.

4. **Document Everything:** Conviction ratings show progress when player feels stuck.

5. **Multiple Exposures:** Need 5-10 behavioral experiments, not just 1-2, to shift beliefs.

6. **Your Role:** Guide the inquiry, don't tell them what to believe. Discovery > instruction.

7. **Integration:** This isn't separate from technical training - beliefs affect execution.

8. **Stay Scientific:** Test beliefs like hypotheses, not moral judgments of player.
